# Disconnection danger

**Belief**: When humans delegate too much to AI agents without maintaining their own understanding, they become unable to act when the agent fails or causes harm. This disconnection is not just a productivity risk — it is a safety risk.

## Components

1. **Acceleration outpaces comprehension** — Agentic AI enables development at speeds where the human stops reading, understanding, and owning the code. Confidence in the agent grows while confidence in one's own ability shrinks.
2. **The paralysis scenario** — When disaster strikes (outage, AI-introduced bug, AI unavailability), the disconnected human cannot even begin to fix the problem. Stress peaks, the brain shuts down. In extreme cases, this endangers the person, not just the system.
3. **Comprehension as safety net** — The only reliable protection is ensuring humans understand what was built. Not after the fact, not in theory — proven understanding, measured and maintained through spaced repetition.
4. **Tools built in calm** — The right response to high-pressure incidents is not improvisation but prepared tools and runbooks, created during relaxed development when learning gates applied. The calm-time investment is the crisis-time safety net.
5. **Learning gates prevent drift** — By tying code changes to demonstrated understanding (Bloom-level gates on PRs), the gap between "what was built" and "what the team understands" never grows unchecked.
