# Mündigkeit

**Goal**: Every person grows toward intellectual self-determination — the ability to think, judge, and act without requiring permission or assistance from their tools.

The German word *Mündigkeit* has no clean English equivalent. It means maturity in the sense of self-governance: a person who is *mündig* can speak for themselves, reason independently, and take responsibility for their own decisions. Kant's famous imperative [*Sapere aude*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapere_aude) — "dare to know" — is a call to Mündigkeit.

ZAM exists because automation threatens Mündigkeit. When an AI handles a task, the person who delegated it becomes less capable of doing it themselves. Over time, the delegation becomes irreversible: the person cannot take over even if they want to. ZAM's purpose is to reverse this dynamic — to ensure that as AI becomes more capable, the humans it serves become more capable too.

## Components

1. **The person must be able to take over** — At any point, in any domain, the human should be able to do what the agent does. This is not efficiency — it is sovereignty. The system measures this via [retention and stability](https://github.com/open-spaced-repetition/fsrs4anki) and plans learning to maintain it.
2. **Competence is earned, not granted** — The system does not declare a person autonomous. The person demonstrates autonomy through repeated successful recall and application. [Symbiosis modes](../beliefs/symbiosis/modes/) are descriptive, not prescriptive.
3. **Practice over quizzing** — Real capability comes from doing, not from answering flashcards. The system creates opportunities for genuine practice, especially for skills at risk of decay. See [Freire's critique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedagogy_of_the_Oppressed) of "banking education" — knowledge deposited into passive recipients is not knowledge at all.
4. **Transparency of process** — The person can inspect everything the system knows about them: their cards, their stability, their review history, their agent's skills. No black boxes. This is the opposite of recommendation algorithms that optimize engagement while hiding their logic.
5. **The right to forget** — Mündigkeit includes choosing what to learn and what to let go. Token deprecation is not failure — it is curation. The person decides what knowledge is worth maintaining.

## Intellectual roots

- [Immanuel Kant, "What is Enlightenment?"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Answering_the_Question:_What_Is_Enlightenment%3F) (1784) — Mündigkeit as the exit from self-imposed immaturity.
- [Paulo Freire, "Pedagogy of the Oppressed"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedagogy_of_the_Oppressed) (1968) — Education as liberation, not deposit. The learner is a subject, not an object.
- [Wilhelm von Humboldt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humboldtian_model_of_higher_education) — Education as self-formation (*Bildung*), not vocational training.
