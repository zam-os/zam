# Where ZAM is going

This folder decomposes the target state of ZAM — not as work items, but as a vision of what the world looks like when ZAM succeeds. Each goal is a facet of that world. Together they describe a society where technology deepens human capability rather than replacing it.

Goals are aspirational. They do not change with every sprint. They change when the project's understanding of what it is trying to achieve evolves.

## Goals

1. **[Mündigkeit](muendigkeit/)** — Every person grows toward intellectual self-determination, not dependence on their tools.
2. **[Bildung through work](bildung-through-work/)** — People form themselves through meaningful tasks, not isolated drills.
3. **[Convivial tools](convivial-tools/)** — Technology amplifies human capability without creating new dependencies.
4. **[Subsidiarity](subsidiarity/)** — Knowledge, data, and decisions belong at the most local level that can handle them.
5. **[Solidarity through capability](solidarity-through-capability/)** — Individual growth serves collective flourishing.

## Relationship to beliefs

[Beliefs](../beliefs/) capture *why* the system works the way it does — the premises about learning, forgetting, and human-AI collaboration. Goals capture *where* the system is heading — the world it is trying to create. Beliefs inform implementation. Goals inform direction.

A belief change cascades to code. A goal change cascades to priorities.

## How to read this folder

Same structure as beliefs: each subfolder states a goal and decomposes it into at most 7 components. Components link to external sources where the idea has been articulated more fully by others. ZAM did not invent these aspirations — it inherits a long tradition and attempts to build tools that serve it.
