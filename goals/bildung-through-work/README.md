# Bildung through work

**Goal**: People form themselves through meaningful tasks, not isolated drills. Learning is not a separate activity from doing — it is an inseparable dimension of doing well.

*Bildung* is the German educational ideal of self-cultivation: not the acquisition of facts, but the formation of a whole person through engagement with knowledge, culture, and challenge. It is distinct from *Ausbildung* (vocational training) in that it shapes judgment and character, not just skill.

ZAM embeds Bildung in work. The parish festival organizer does not attend a class on volunteer coordination — they coordinate volunteers with an agent that ensures they understand *why* each approach works, not just *what* to do next. The knowledge becomes theirs. This is the [dual education model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_education_system) generalized beyond the workshop: every task is a training session, every agent is a Meister.

## Components

1. **The task is the curriculum** — Learning goals emerge from what the person is actually trying to accomplish, not from a pre-designed syllabus. Tokens are registered because the task requires them, not because a schedule says it is time. See [John Dewey's experiential learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experiential_learning).
2. **Context strengthens memory** — "I learned this while deploying production" creates a stronger trace than "flashcard #47." The session model captures this context. This is supported by [encoding specificity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encoding_specificity_principle) and [situated cognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Situated_cognition).
3. **Depth over breadth** — [Bloom's taxonomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloom%27s_taxonomy) structures the journey from remembering a fact to synthesizing novel solutions. The system does not treat all knowledge as equal — it tracks cognitive depth and teaches accordingly.
4. **The Gesellenstück principle** — In the German guild tradition, a journeyman (*Geselle*) proves mastery by creating a [masterpiece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Masterpiece) independently. ZAM's autonomy mode is this principle: the system steps back, the person executes, and the work itself demonstrates competence.
5. **Repetition is not punishment** — Spaced repetition revisits knowledge at the moment of optimal challenge — neither too easy (boring) nor too hard (discouraging). This is [desirable difficulty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desirable_difficulty), not rote repetition. Each review is a chance to understand more deeply than last time.

## Intellectual roots

- [John Dewey, "Democracy and Education"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy_and_Education) (1916) — Learning through doing. Education is not preparation for life; it is life.
- [German dual education system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_education_system) — Theory and practice interleaved. The workshop is the classroom.
- [Lev Vygotsky, Zone of Proximal Development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zone_of_proximal_development) — The space between what a learner can do alone and what they can do with guidance. The agent lives in this zone.
